Lasagna Recipe

Description

Lasagna is a classic Italian baked pasta dish consisting of wide, flat sheets layered with fillings such as ragù, béchamel sauce, vegetables, and various cheeses. The name derives from the Latin lasanum (cooking pot) or Greek laganum (flatbread), with ancient origins tracing back to Etruscan frescoes and Roman cookbooks, though the modern form is codified by the Accademia Italiana della Cucina as a Bolognese specialty of spinach egg pasta, ragù, and béchamel.

The dish exhibits significant regional diversity across Italy, with southern versions typically featuring dried pasta, ricotta, mozzarella, and meat ragù, while northern variations use fresh egg pasta and white sauces. Holiday traditions also shape the recipe, including Sicilian sciabbó with chocolate and pork, or Friulian lasagne da Fornel, a meatless Christmas Eve dish layered with apples, nuts, and dried fruit.

Ingredients

  • Noodles: 12 to 14 lasagna noodles (boiled or oven-ready)
  • Meat Sauce: 1 lb ground beef and/or 1 lb Italian sausage, 1 onion (chopped), 3 – 4 cloves garlic (minced), 28 – 48 oz crushed tomatoes or marinara sauce, 2 tbsp tomato paste, 1 tsp dried basil, 1 tsp dried oregano, salt, and black pepper
  • Cheese Filling: 15 – 32 oz ricotta cheese, 1 large egg, ¼ cup chopped parsley, ½ cup grated Parmesan cheese, salt, and pepper
  • Topping: 4 cups shredded mozzarella cheese and ½ cup grated Parmesan cheese

Steps

  1. Prep: Preheat oven to 375°F (190°C). Boil noodles according to package directions if not using oven-ready varieties; drain and set aside.
  2. Make Sauce: Brown the ground beef and/or sausage in a large skillet with onions and garlic; drain excess fat. Stir in tomatoes, tomato paste, and herbs; simmer for 10 – 20 minutes.
  3. Make Filling: In a bowl, mix ricotta cheese, egg, parsley, Parmesan, salt, and pepper until combined.
  4. Layer: Spread a thin layer of meat sauce in the bottom of a 9x13 inch baking dish. Add a layer of noodles, followed by ⅓ of the ricotta mixture, ⅓ of the remaining sauce, and a sprinkle of mozzarella. Repeat layers twice.
  5. Top: Finish with a final layer of noodles, the remaining meat sauce, and the rest of the mozzarella and Parmesan cheeses.
  6. Bake: Cover tightly with foil (sprayed with oil to prevent sticking) and bake for 40–45 minutes. Remove foil and bake uncovered for an additional 15–20 minutes until bubbly and golden.
  7. Rest: Let the lasagna stand for 15 minutes before slicing to allow layers to set.
